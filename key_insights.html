Teaching Agents to "Look Before Leaping"

Core Thesis

We found that standard supervised imitation learning data is insufficient.

  1. Models require learning not just the optimal path, but how to explore the environment
  2. This work aims to verify the value of inducing information-revealing behaviors during fine-tuning

Rigorous Validation (Mental Rotation 3D)

The study further demonstrated that gains are not simply due to longer trajectories10. When continued training was performed on baseline demonstrations after the model was fine-tuned with information-revealing data, performance deteriorated11.

Final Insight: The observed improvements stem from the informative structure of the demonstrations rather than quantity or length12. This confirms that inducing state-disambiguating behaviors is essential for effective supervised fine-tuning13.
