Diverse, Customizable, Scalable Environments for Multimodal Agents

VisGym consists of 17 diverse, long-horizon environments designed to systematically evaluate, diagnose, and train VLMs on visually interactive tasks. The agent must select each action conditioned on both its past actions and observation history.

Abstract

Modern Vision–Language Models (VLMs) remain poorly characterized in multi-step visual interactions, particularly in how they integrate perception, memory, and action over long horizons.

We introduce VisGym, a comprehensive gymnasium of 17 diverse environments designed to systematically evaluate and train VLMs on visually interactive tasks. The suite spans multiple domains—including symbolic puzzles, real-image understanding, navigation, and manipulation—and provides flexible controls over difficulty, input representation, planning horizon, and feedback mechanisms. We also provide multi-step solvers that generate structured demonstrations, enabling supervised finetuning approaches.

Our comprehensive evaluations reveal that all frontier models struggle significantly in interactive settings, achieving low success rates in both easy (26.8%) and hard (12.6%) configurations. Through detailed experiments, we identify several critical limitations: models fail to effectively leverage long context, performing worse with unbounded history than with truncated windows. Additionally, we find that several text-based symbolic tasks become substantially harder once rendered visually, highlighting the unique challenges of visual reasoning.

However, we demonstrate that explicit goal observations, textual feedback, and exploratory demonstrations in partially observable or unknown-dynamics settings yield consistent improvements through supervised finetuning, revealing concrete failure modes and actionable pathways for advancing multi-step visual decision-making. All code, data, and models will be publicly released.

Frontier VLMs Fail on VisGym

Even the best-performing frontier model, GPT-5, achieves only 26.43% on VisGym (Easy) and 12.57% on VisGym (Hard), indicating that VisGym poses a significant challenge for existing models.
